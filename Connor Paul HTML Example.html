Main Argument

During the Renaissance time period the processes of making books, tapestries, and woodcuts were very time and resource consuming. Alongside this fact, authors, especially those who were female, often found themselves having a hard time finding and getting in contact with audiences for their work. The lack of women's rights, the slow printing process, the absence of a fast and easy way to produce copies, and not having the Internet all played huge roles in slowing the spread of books, tapestries and woodcuts and many other forms of media. Modern digitalization and technology have vastly increased the speed of book and tapestry making, allowed authors to find audiences more quickly, replaced woodcuts and created entirely new forms of media.



Renaissance Literature

Book making in the Renaissance time period was not an easy process. Creating a book took many hours of slow work, as well as a lot of money because in the beginning of the Renaissance all books had to be hand written. To get a book made you would have to be a writer of high status or a very wealthy individual that could afford the process. As you can imagine, there weren't many books in circulation at the time due to the difficulties of the creation process. These issues created a need for a change.

Around the year 1440 Gutenberg invented the printing press and literature drastically changed forever. The printing press allowed books to be made much faster and much easier. Print shops began to pop up throughout Europe and authors began to produce more work. Although the printing press sped up the process, certain authors still had a hard time reaching their intended audiences. Books were still expensive and it was harder for people of the middle class to get ahold of them without the necessary funds to purchase one or have one made. Print shops also often only focused on popular authors at the time unless someone requested a different book for himself or herself. This led to primarily male authors being the most published and most book collections being held by the upper class. Authors that were searching for a middle class audience had a very hard time getting their work circulated because there was no guaranteed way to get the writing to these people. Women authors especially had a hard time getting their work published because during the Renaissance time period there were no women's rights and women were not treated the same as men. They were expected to tend to household duties and entertain guests of the house, not work as writers. This led to many women being denied publication, or not writing at all. These women and less popular authors needed to find a way to reach their audiences.

(Pictured above from left to right: Gutenberg printing press animation, letterpress composing stick, traditional paper making process animation.)

Overtime women, like Amelia Lanyer, Isabella Whitney, and Esther Inglis, figured out solutions to their audience problems. Women authors in the Renaissance began to focus their writing on flattery, dedications, and widely known subjects. For example, Amelia Lanyer is well known for tailoring dedications in her books in order to flatter certain political and wealthy figures; this is prominent in Salve Deus Rex Judeorum in the section To the Queenes most Excellent Majestie. This encouraged these well known people to ask for copies to be made and distributed. Lanyer also used widely known topics such as Roman mythology in order to give readers a frame of reference that most already knew about. People could better understand and interpret what she was writing and that encouraged more people to want her books. Isabella Whitney liked to try and share connections with her readers. She published many written letters in which she talks about her struggles as a woman in desperate need of help. This approach proved to be very helpful because thousands of people could relate to her struggle and draw similarities between their lives and hers. This made Whitney's writing much more personal to the reader. She also took into consideration the public taste of the times and used that knowledge combined with a satirical tone to produce poems and other literature that were amusing to read. Esther Inglis found her niche in making personalizations and gift books with the help of her husband. She used her husband's career as a clerk in order to get her talents circulated. When her husband would deliver something to someone of importance, he would give him or her a gift book made by Esther along with some personalizations of whatever was being delivered. This allowed Esther's talents to spread throughout Europe.

Other notable attempts at reaching audiences were made by men. For example, the famous Edmund Spenser was having trouble getting his works published and circulated. In a bold move he created The Shepheardes Calender that mimicked the almanacs of the time and included woodcuts. Almanacs were widely popular during the Renaissance time period, and by simply changing the look and feel of his work, Spenser was able to enter the mainstream and become more well known.

The Effect of Modern Technology and Digitalization on Writing

Nowadays with the Internet, the search for an audience has drastically changed. Women and other writers no longer need to struggle to find an audience, they only have to produce something and post it in the correct area. For example, there are thousands of websites and blogs geared towards women such as BlogHer and Live Your Dream Blogs For Women. Here women talk about all aspects of life and share their experiences and views. Women like Valerie Maltoni and Tamar Weinberg were able to become famous very quickly through online blogging. Other popular options women and authors have used are writing for online magazines, newspapers, and journals. This approach allows writers to reach millions of people in a more professional style. No matter the approach taken, it is incredibly easy to become an online writer.

The reason it is so easy to become a writer and reach an audience by using the Internet is because the only things one needs to post online is some type of device and an Internet connection. Both of these don't even need to be owned by the writer because it is easy to find public machines to access the Internet and even easier to find a Wi-Fi connection. If writing things on the Internet doesn't interest an author that does not mean that they will not reach an audience. Devices like the Kindle and apps like iBooks allow consumers to easily access and read thousands of different books and other literature media with the tap of a finger.

Not only has the reading interface changed, but the book printing experience has changed as well. In the past writers had to go to a print shop, convince the printer to buy one of his or her manuscripts, convince the printer to turn the manuscript into a physical book, and encourage the printer to print and sell this book as soon as they could. The author would then most often be paid close to nothing, if anything at all; the print shop would receive all sales and profits. This process was tedious, annoying and unfair and we can assume that because of this, many writers were denied publication or avoided it altogether. Nowadays Amazon has changed the printing experience completely. A writer can go onto Amazon's publishing website, upload a Word document of their book, collaborate with trained professionals to edit the work, and pick out font styles and cover images. Once they are satisfied with the look and feel of their book, Amazon will have as many copies made as the author wants and will even sell them on the Amazon Marketplace. The author will also receive most of the profits from the sale of his or her book. All of these changes to the writing, printing, sharing, and publishing of a book have aided the spread and accessibility of literature in the 21st century and have allowed anyone to become an author if they so desire.



Renaissance Tapestries

Tapestries were a very intricate form of textile art throughout the Renaissance time period. In simple terms, a tapestry is a large blanket like textile that people hang on their walls, use as curtains, and/or use as a throw. During the Renaissance, tapestries were mostly used as decorative pieces to hang on the walls while also providing a type of insulation to keep the house warm and to prevent echoes. Tapestry making was a very long and slow process that could take months to finish. Due to their size and weave difficulty, tapestries were almost always made by more than one weaver at a time. Most of the time there were at least five weavers working at a time, with some tapestries requiring thirty or more weavers. For this reason, there are no well-known names associated with tapestries, only shop names or the names of the houses where they were found. For example, a set of four tapestries all depicting different hunting scenes is referred to as the Devonshire Hunting Tapestries because they originally came from the estate of the Dukes of Devonshire. Another example of this unknown artist issue is a set of seven tapestries all showing different scenes of a unicorn hunt; the set is referred to as the Unicorn Tapestries.

Although there aren't records of famous tapestry weavers, that doesn't mean that people in the Renaissance didn't know about tapestries. Tapestries were widely popular among the rich and religious officials. Renaissance tapestries were amazing works of art, and having one showed wealth and status. Some people even went so far as to request their tapestry to be made with silver and gold-wrapped silk. The tapestry shops themselves did not have many problems reaching an audience because once people saw a tapestry they wanted one. Although this is true, the middle class was mostly an untapped audience due to the cost of acquiring a tapestry. All of this changed once the age of technology and digitalization came around.




The Effect of Modern Technology on Tapestries

Nowadays tapestries are very different than what they used to be. Most tapestries today are made via screen-printing or someone painting a design on the surface (painting a tapestry was actually a reason to be jailed during the Renaissance). Most tapestries are also no longer woven by hand and instead are woven with the help of machines. Machines help the modern weaver complete the process much faster than in the past. If you think about a bed sheet, a tapestry is made the same way today.

The Internet has also helped tapestry weavers reach a wider audience. Modern technology has reduced the cost of the making process as well as the materials, which makes tapestries relatively cheap. This allows everyone to be able to acquire a tapestry if they want one. The acquisition process has also changed as well. Tapestry weavers use the Internet to display and sell their pieces. Online sites such as Society6 and Etsy sell thousands of tapestries; some handmade and some machine made and machine printed. Now people simply access the web, press buy, type in a credit card number and the tapestry they wanted shows up at their front doorstep within days. The ease of this process allows tapestry companies and weavers to sell to everyone.



Renaissance Woodcuts

Visual images in the Renaissance became popular very quickly. This demand for visual images gave life to things like woodcuts, which became highly desired by both readers and certain authors. Woodcuts are a very individualized art form that served as beautiful epitexts when printers stamped the image into a book. Each woodcut is one of a kind because every cut onto the wood is unique in its own way and will never be replicated. Even with woodcut templates and drawn images, woodcut artists could never create an exact replica of another woodcut.

The presence of a printed illustration encouraged people to buy books, so printers quickly started adding them to certain texts and authors started requesting specific woodcuts to be used in their texts as well. These printed images made the texts much more valuable to the reader and the printer could have charged more for books that included them. Woodcut artists did not have a hard time finding an audience at all, but they were not recognized for their work. Often times, woodcuts would be added to books without any citation of who the original artist was. Other artist and printers would also make copies of original woodcuts and begin to use them. Nowadays copyright laws prevent things like this from happening.

The Effect of Modern Technology on Woodcuts

In the 21st century humans as a whole upload and share over 1.8 billion images a day, making them a very important roll in the day-to-day lives of those living in developed countries. Images are an ever-increasing part of life, and new technologies have been created in order to produce images in books. Images are now printed into books by simply uploading the image into a document and having that printed with a modern printer. The need for woodcuts has vanished, and woodcutting has become a hobby of sorts. Nowadays 3D printing and laser cutting have become the modern woodcut. 3D objects can be printed easily and laser cut images take no time at all. 3D printing and laser cutting are also done completely by machines and humans don't even need to oversee the process. Although both are not, 3D objects and laser cut images could theoretically be used in the same way that woodcuts were in the past. We don't use them like we once used woodcuts because modern printers do a way better job.
